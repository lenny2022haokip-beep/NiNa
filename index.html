The Kuki Weavers

The Kuki community resides in the rolling hills of Manipur and Northeast India. For generations, weaving has been a form of literature. Without written texts, historic events, nature motifs, and tribe lineages were woven directly into fabrics as detailed geometric grids.

Every cloth we produce is handloomed by women working from their home courtyards under the sun. This model allows them to remain anchors of their families while earning a fair, independent wage that values their intellectual heritage.

Ancestral Mathematics

Unlike power looms, the Kuki backstrap (or loin) loom requires no electricity. The weaver sits on the floor with a leather back strap, using her body weight to control the tension of the cotton warp threads. Every design is calculated line by line from memory.

By purchasing from **NiNa**, you are direct participants in the preservation of this heritage. We ensure that 100% of our production funds go directly back to the weaving households, ensuring this math is passed to another generation.

Living Heritage

The Loom of Generations

The weaving tradition of the Kuki people is a living archive. Our three signature patterns — **Saipikhup** (elephant footprint), **Thangnang** (lattice repeat), and **Mongvom** (layered diamond rows) — represent a visual language spoken in thread.

Our collaboration with master weavers translates these heavy wraps into modern clothing items, lightweight scarves, bags, and collectibles that carry ancestral history into the global marketplace.

Featured Artisans

Meet the master craftsmen keeping our ancient designs alive in Manipur.

Master Weaver

Nengzamoi Haokip

Churachandpur, Manipur

With over 35 years of backstrap loom experience, Nengzamoi is our repository of the sacred Saipikhup pattern, teaching young village girls the mathematical precision of counting threads.

Loom Innovator

Kimkhosei Touthang

Lamka, Manipur

Kimkhosei designs our contemporary home textile ranges, modifying traditional loin loom structures to weave wider fabrics without losing the tight density characteristic of hill textiles.

Beadwork Artisan

Ginzalian Mate

Moreh, Manipur

Ginzalian gathers carnelian, river-stones, and locally cast brass to assemble our jewelry collection. His work blends traditional symbols with clean, modern styling.
